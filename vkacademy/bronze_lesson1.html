The 1% Warning

Imagine your phone screen flashes a **1% Battery** warning. What do you do? You don't get angry at the phone. You don't try to force it to run heavy games. You look for a charger.

Yet, when your own body and mind flash a warning light, you do the exact opposite. You tell yourself: "Just push through it. Keep running."

Burnout isn't a sign that you are weak. It's just your system shutting down because you ignored the warning lights.

Rule 1: Respect Yourself

Under the Ordo mindset, respecting yourself doesn't mean spa days or buying things. It means something much harder: **Ruthless Self-Honesty**.

It means looking in the mirror and admitting your true capacity. If you only have 20% left to give today, trying to pretend you have 100% isn't brave. It's a lie.

To unlock the armor, we have to clear the noise. Choose the statement that hits closest to home right now:

I am carrying too many things that aren't my job.
I am afraid of letting people down if I say no.

Why We Do This

Why did we just make you write down that heavy thing? Because look at it carefully.

That task is external to you. It is a puzzle to be solved, not an identity. You have been treating it like a life-or-death situation, allowing your internal peace to shatter over an outside event.

By practicing **Self-Honesty**, your mind draws a line between Who You Are and What You Are Doing. That line is your suit of armor.
